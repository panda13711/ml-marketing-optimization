## 5ï¸âƒ£ POTENTIAL CONFOUNDERS & RISKS

### Critical Factors That May Compromise Analysis

#### HIGH IMPACT Confounders

**1. Seasonality & Timing** ðŸ”´
- **Issue:** Campaigns may have run during different periods
- **Risk:** Performance differences could be timing-related, not platform-related
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Analyze campaign launch dates
  - Control for seasonal effects
  - Test same campaigns across different seasons

**2. Product Mix Variation** ðŸ”´
- **Issue:** Platforms attract different product categories (proven in analysis)
- **Risk:** ROI differences may reflect product margins, not platform effectiveness
- **Current Evidence:** Instagram = 60% Fashion, LinkedIn = 89% Tech
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Normalize ROI by product category
  - Test cross-platform with same product category

**3. Ad Creative Quality** ðŸ”´
- **Issue:** Same ad may not be appropriate for all platforms
- **Risk:** Poor performance may reflect creative mismatch, not platform viability
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Develop platform-specific creative (see recommendations)
  - A/B test native vs cross-posted content

**4. Attribution Window Issues** ðŸ”´
- **Issue:** Last-touch attribution may miss multi-touch customer journeys
- **Risk:** Instagram may get credit for sales influenced by earlier touchpoints
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Implement multi-touch attribution modeling
  - Track customer journey across platforms

#### MEDIUM IMPACT Confounders

**5. Audience Overlap** ðŸŸ¡
- Same customers exposed to multiple campaigns
- May artificially inflate apparent conversion rates

**6. Budget Level Non-linearity** ðŸŸ¡
- $1K vs $2K vs $3K campaigns may have different efficiency curves
- Need to test incremental budget increases

**7. Audience Maturity Stage** ðŸŸ¡
- Different platforms may catch customers at different buying stages
- LinkedIn may be awareness, Instagram may be conversion

**8. External Market Factors** ðŸŸ¡
- Economic conditions, competitor actions, trends
- Need competitive intelligence and market context
